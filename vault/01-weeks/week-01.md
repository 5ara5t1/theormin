# Week 1 — Phase 1 cold start (2026-05-26 to 2026-06-01)

**Budget:** 30 hours
**Phase:** 1 (Mathematical foundation)
**Streams active:** Real analysis (primary), linear algebra (secondary). Nothing else yet.
**Strategic call:** Proof technique is the binding constraint. Tao builds it deliberately; Zorich runs in parallel as the harder text you'll grow into. Strang grounds linear algebra geometrically before formalism.

| Stream | Hours | What |

| Linear algebra — Strang + MIT 18.06 lectures | 8 | Parallel, lighter |

## Daily plan

### Day 1 (Tue 5/26) — Setup + first analysis contact (4 hrs)

- **Infrastructure (2 hrs):**
  - Set up a derivations notebook (paper recommended for math; the physical act of writing matters). Separate page-counted sections for: chapter summaries, worked problems, reprove-from-memory log.
  - Start a problems-worked log. Aim is ~1,500 problems through Phase 1 — track running count from day 1.
  - Pick a fixed daily start time. Cold start has no rhythm yet; impose one.
- **Tao Analysis I, Ch. 1 (Introduction) (2 hrs):** Read carefully. Don't skip the "why analysis?" motivation — you'll forget it later and the framing matters.

### Day 2 (Wed 5/27) — Tao Ch. 2 begins (5 hrs)

- **Tao Ch. 2 §2.1–2.2 (3 hrs):** Peano axioms and addition. Read with pen in hand; rewrite proofs in your own words.
- **Strang, Ch. 1 (2 hrs):** Vectors, dot products, geometry. Read alongside MIT 18.06 Lecture 1 (~50 min on YouTube — free). Strang lecturing is high-bandwidth; the book alone undersells him.

### Day 3 (Thu 5/28) — Tao + first Demidovich (5 hrs)

- **Tao §2.2 exercises (2.5 hrs):** Work every exercise. They feel elementary; that's the point. You're building the reflex of writing valid proofs about objects you already "know."
- **Demidovich §1 (real numbers, inequalities), 10 problems (2 hrs):** Calibration set. Don't try to crush. Note which problems took >20 min — those signal gaps to revisit.
- **Strang Ch. 2 §2.1–2.2 + MIT 18.06 Lecture 2 (0.5 hrs lecture + half hour reading):** Solving linear equations, elimination.

### Day 4 (Fri 5/29) — Tao Ch. 2 finish + Zorich first contact (5 hrs)

- **Tao §2.3 (multiplication) + exercises (2.5 hrs):** Same pattern. Notice how the proofs from §2.2 chain into §2.3.
- **Zorich vol 1, Ch. 1 §1–2 (sets, logic, functions) (2 hrs):** Read, don't problem-set. Zorich's notation and rigor will feel sharper than Tao's. Goal is exposure, not mastery yet.
- **Strang MIT 18.06 Lecture 3 + reading (0.5 hrs):** Matrix multiplication and inverses.

### Day 5 (Sat 5/30) — Long block: linear algebra deep dive (6 hrs)

- **Strang Ch. 2 + MIT 18.06 Lectures 4–6 (4 hrs):** Permutations, A=LU, transposes, vector spaces. Watch the lectures in 1.5x if comfortable; the production is from the 2000s but the teaching is timeless.
- **Strang Ch. 2 problems (2 hrs):** Work 8–12 problems. Linear algebra problems are usually computational at this stage; build the manipulation reflex.

### Day 6 (Sun 5/31) — Reset day: Zorich + Demidovich + slack (4 hrs)

- **Zorich Ch. 1 §3 (elementary functions, real numbers via Cauchy sequences or Dedekind cuts — Zorich's construction is dense) (1.5 hrs):** Read carefully. You may need to reread.
- **Demidovich, 8 more problems from §1–2 (1.5 hrs):** Push toward limit-of-sequences problems if comfortable. If §1 still feels rough, stay there.
- **Slack (1 hr):** Catch up on anything that spilled over from earlier in the week. Resist the urge to "get ahead."

### Day 7 (Mon 6/1) — Verification day (1 hr)

- **Reprove from memory (45 min):** Pick the most non-trivial proof from Tao §2.2 (e.g., "addition is commutative on the natural numbers, by induction"). Reproduce on a blank page. Don't peek. If you can't get it, that's the diagnostic — re-read and repeat next week.
- **Week summary (15 min):** Write 1 page in your notebook: what did you learn, what felt hard, what to revisit. This is week 1 of your personal compressed Landau-Lifshitz; format and habit matter more than content this early.

---

## What NOT to do this week

- **Don't open Arnold ODE, Markushevich, Needham, or Gelfand-Fomin.** They will be there in Week 5+.
- **Don't open Axler.** It lands after Strang has given you geometric intuition. Premature axiomatic linear algebra teaches notation, not understanding.
- **Don't start physics.** Phase 1 is math only. Irodov / Sivukhin enter in Phase 2.
- **Don't use AI to verify proofs.** Verify yourself by reproducing on a blank page. Bounded AI use was specified in the curriculum for a reason.
- **Don't try to "catch up" if you fall behind.** Recalibrate the next week. The pace is what gets adjusted, not the quality of work.

## Looking ahead
