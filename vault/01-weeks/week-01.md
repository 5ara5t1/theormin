## Daily plan

### Day 1 (Tue 5/26) — 4 hrs

- Infrastructure (2 hrs):
  - Paper notebook with sections for chapter summaries, worked problems, reprove-from-memory log.
  - Problems-worked log; track running count from Day 1.
  - Pick a fixed daily start time.
- Tao Ch. 1, Introduction (2 hrs). Read carefully. The motivation in Ch. 1 — why analysis needs rigorous foundations — sets up the rest of the book.

### Day 2 (Wed 5/27) — 5 hrs

- Tao Ch. 2 §2.1–2.2, Peano axioms and addition (3 hrs). Rewrite proofs in your own words.
- Strang Ch. 1 + MIT 18.06 Lecture 1 (2 hrs). Vectors, dot products, geometry.

### Day 3 (Thu 5/28) — 5 hrs

- Tao §2.2 exercises (2.5 hrs). Work every exercise.
- Demidovich §1, 10 problems (2 hrs). Real numbers and inequalities. Track which problems took >20 min.
- Strang Ch. 2 §2.1–2.2 + MIT 18.06 Lecture 2 (0.5 hrs).

### Day 4 (Fri 5/29) — 5 hrs

- Tao §2.3 + exercises (2.5 hrs). Multiplication.
- Zorich Ch. 1 §1–2 (2 hrs). Read only; no problems yet.
- Strang MIT 18.06 Lecture 3 + reading (0.5 hrs).

### Day 5 (Sat 5/30) — 6 hrs

- Strang Ch. 2 + MIT 18.06 Lectures 4–6 (4 hrs). Permutations, A=LU, transposes, vector spaces.
- Strang Ch. 2 problems, 8–12 (2 hrs).

### Day 6 (Sun 5/31) — 4 hrs

- Zorich Ch. 1 §3 (1.5 hrs). Real numbers via Cauchy sequences / Dedekind cuts.
- Demidovich §1–2, 8 problems (1.5 hrs).
- Slack (1 hr). Catch up on anything that spilled over.

### Day 7 (Mon 6/1) — 1 hr

- Reprove from memory (45 min). Pick a non-trivial proof from Tao §2.2 (e.g., "addition is commutative on N"). Blank page, no peeking. If stuck, that's the diagnostic.
- Week summary (15 min). One page in the notebook.
